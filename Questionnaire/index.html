Question 16

Block Elements: Starting on a new line and extending the full width of their parent container. They stack vertically, and elements before and after them are displayed on different lines. Inline Elements: Do not start on a new line and only take up as much width as necessary. They do not force a new line before or after themselves. Inline-Block Elements: Combine features of both block and inline elements. They generate a block-level box in the document flow but allow other elements to sit beside them on the same line.
